# FAQ — What Zartis Doesn't Do

## Do you do AI research or academic work?

No. Zartis is an engineering and consulting company, not a research lab. The work is applied: production systems, real workflows, business problems with measurable outcomes. If the goal is to publish a paper or push the frontier of ML research, Zartis is not the right partner.

## Do you build off-the-shelf AI products we can just plug in?

No. Zartis builds custom systems for specific client problems. There's no pre-packaged product to license. The value is in the engineering and delivery, not a SaaS subscription.

## Do you do low-code or no-code implementations?

Zartis works in code. The team is engineers. If a client's needs are well-served by a no-code tool, we'll say so — but that's not what Zartis is for.

## Can you help with AI strategy without any engineering involvement?

There's a limit to purely advisory engagements. Zartis is strongest when strategy connects directly to technical delivery. A roadmap without the engineering depth to validate it is less useful than one built by people who've shipped the systems it describes. That said, a scoping engagement or a workshop can be a valid starting point if the engineering investment follows.

## Do you handle managed operations or 24/7 support?

Not as a primary offering. The model is delivery — Zartis builds and hands off. Ongoing operational support can be structured in certain cases, but it's not the default, and it's not an operations centre model.

## What if our project is outside your core areas?

Zartis' core is AI engineering, software engineering, and technology consulting. If a request falls outside that — specialised hardware, physical systems, non-technology domains — we'll say so directly rather than take on work where the team isn't the right fit.
