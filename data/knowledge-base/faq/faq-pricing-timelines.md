# FAQ — Pricing and Timelines

## How much does it cost to work with Zartis?

We don't publish fixed rates because the cost depends entirely on the scope, team size, seniority mix, and duration. A two-week discovery with one AI engineer is a different conversation than a six-month embedded team across three product squads. A number without that context isn't useful — it's either too high and puts people off before the conversation starts, or too low and creates expectations that don't survive the scoping call. The right first step is a short conversation. From there we can give a figure that actually means something.

## How long does a typical engagement take?

It varies significantly by type. For AI features and RAG pipelines, a production-ready delivery is typically 6–12 weeks. More complex systems involving data platforms, agentic architectures, or MLOps components can run 3–6 months. Team extension engagements are ongoing by definition — they continue as long as they're delivering value. We scope before we estimate. The first output of any serious engagement is a shared understanding of what done looks like.

## Do you work on a fixed price or time and materials?

Both, depending on the engagement. Early-stage and exploratory work usually runs on time and materials — the scope isn't stable enough for a fixed price to be meaningful. Well-defined deliverables with clear success criteria can be fixed price. We'll recommend the structure that makes sense for the specific situation. If the scope isn't defined enough for a fixed price, we'll say so rather than give a number that isn't real.

## What's the minimum engagement size?

There's no formal minimum, but very short or very small engagements tend to produce limited value relative to the onboarding overhead. The engagements that work best — for both sides — tend to have a clear problem, enough scope for real impact, and enough duration for the team to get into a productive rhythm. A scoping call is the right way to figure out if there's a fit.

## How quickly can a team start?

Faster than most people expect for the right profile. For common engineering and AI roles where Zartis has existing bench depth, introductions can happen within a week. For highly specialised profiles, the timeline depends on what's available. We don't place people who aren't right for the engagement just to fill a slot quickly.
