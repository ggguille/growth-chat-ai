# FAQ — How Zartis Works

## How does the engagement process start?

A scoping call. Not a sales call — a conversation to understand the problem, the team structure, the tech stack, and what kind of engagement makes sense. The output is a shared understanding of the situation, not a proposal with numbers attached. Proposals come after scoping, not before.

## Do Zartis engineers work embedded in our team or separately?

Embedded is the default. Zartis engineers work on the client's codebase, follow the client's processes, attend the client's meetings, and use the client's tools. From the perspective of the day-to-day team, a Zartis engineer should feel like a well-prepared new hire, not an external vendor.

For end-to-end delivery engagements, the model is slightly different — Zartis owns the delivery and reports against agreed milestones, but there's still direct collaboration with the client team throughout.

## What happens if an engineer isn't working out?

We replace them. The model only works if the right people are in place. If an engineer isn't the right fit technically or culturally, we address it. There's no bureaucratic process — it's a direct conversation and a fast resolution.

## Do you work with our existing tools and stack, or do you impose your own?

Yours. Zartis engineers are there to deliver within the client's environment. We don't impose process or tooling changes unless asked to advise on them. If we see something that we think would materially improve how the team works, we'll say so — but whether to act on it is the client's call.

## What does the handoff look like at the end of an engagement?

For end-to-end delivery, the handoff is planned from the start. Documentation, knowledge transfer, and a transition period where the client's team takes over with Zartis still available to answer questions. The goal is a system and a team that can operate independently.

For team extension, there's no formal end — the engagement tapers as needed. If the client decides to hire directly, we'll help make that transition smooth.

## Do you work with startups or only larger companies?

Both. The client list spans Series B startups (like DataShield, acquired by ClearGuard) through to large enterprise clients (Voltara / Meridian Energy Group, RegEdge). The right engagement structure differs by stage — what a 30-person startup needs from a partner is different from what a 500-person enterprise needs — but the quality standard is the same.
