# Engagement Model: Team Extension

## What It Is

Team extension means Zartis engineers become part of the client's engineering organisation. They work on the client's codebase, follow the client's processes, attend the client's standups and sprint ceremonies, and are indistinguishable from internal hires in day-to-day operation.

This is not staff augmentation in the traditional sense — it is not a body-shopping model where headcount is filled with whoever is available. The model is built on matching the right engineers to the right problem and integrating them properly into the team.

## When It Makes Sense

- The client has strong product and engineering direction but lacks capacity or specific expertise.
- The client wants to scale a capability (e.g. AI engineering, data pipelines) without the overhead of full-time hiring.
- The client needs people who can start contributing quickly and work as peers with the existing team.
- The initiative is ongoing — not a fixed-scope project that ends at delivery.

## How It Works

1. **Scoping call** — understand the team structure, tech stack, problem, and what kind of engineer is needed.
2. **Candidate matching** — Zartis identifies engineers who match the technical profile and team culture.
3. **Introductions** — client interviews candidates before any commitment.
4. **Onboarding** — Zartis engineers join the client's team and start contributing. Typically ramp time is measured in days, not weeks, because the people placed are senior.
5. **Ongoing** — the engagement continues as long as it's delivering value. There's no lock-in.

## Typical Profile

Senior engineers. People who have solved this class of problem before. The expectation is that a Zartis engineer can be given a ticket on day two and ship something useful by the end of the sprint.

## Commercial Structure

Usually time and materials. A day rate per engineer, agreed at the start of the engagement. No surprise costs. Engagement can scale up or down as needs change.
