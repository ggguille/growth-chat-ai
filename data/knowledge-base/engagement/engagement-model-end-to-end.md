# Engagement Model: End-to-End Delivery

## What It Is

End-to-end delivery means Zartis takes ownership of a defined system or capability from design through to production. The client owns the outcome and the final product. Zartis owns the delivery — architecture, engineering, testing, deployment, and handoff.

## When It Makes Sense

- The client has a well-defined problem but limited internal engineering resource to solve it.
- The client wants a working system delivered, not engineers to manage.
- The scope is bounded enough to define success criteria upfront.
- The client wants to own and maintain the system after delivery — the handoff is built into the engagement.

## How It Works

1. **Scoping** — detailed discovery to understand the problem, constraints, and success criteria. This is not optional — a proposal without scope is a guess.
2. **Architecture design** — Zartis proposes the technical approach. The client reviews and approves.
3. **Delivery** — engineering, testing, and deployment, with regular client checkpoints throughout.
4. **Handoff** — documentation, knowledge transfer, and support during transition to client ownership. The goal is a system the client's team can maintain independently.

## Typical Engagements

- Building an AI feature or RAG pipeline into an existing product
- Designing and delivering a data platform from scratch
- Implementing an agentic system end-to-end
- MVP development for a new product or capability

## Commercial Structure

Fixed price for well-defined scope. Time and materials for exploratory or evolving scope. The commercial structure is chosen to match the risk profile of the engagement, not to maximise billing. If the scope isn't well-defined enough for a fixed price, Zartis will say so.

## After Delivery

The default assumption is that the client's team takes ownership after handoff. Zartis is not trying to create dependency. If an ongoing support or evolution engagement makes sense, that's a separate conversation.
