# AI Expertise Meets Domain Knowledge: Accelerating AI Adoption at RegEdge

## The Client

RegEdge is a B2B SaaS company that helps organisations manage product compliance and regulatory requirements. They have deep domain expertise and subject matter experts (SMEs) in regulatory content.

## The Problem

The company wanted to accelerate AI adoption across their platform but recognised that internal AI engineering depth was the missing piece. In the words of Sarah Fleming, VP of Engineering:

> "We started thinking, if we want to accelerate with bigger projects, let's bring in some of the AI expertise in Zartis. We're in a great position in Compliance and Risks because we have a lot of SMEs in our own business. It's a beautiful complementary relationship because we can bring in the high-level AI expertise from Zartis to work with our SMEs."

The challenge was bridging the gap between deep regulatory domain knowledge (which lived inside the company) and production-grade AI engineering (which did not).

## What Zartis Delivered

Zartis provided embedded AI engineers who worked directly with RegEdge's internal SMEs. The model was explicitly complementary: the client owned the domain knowledge, Zartis owned the AI engineering. Together the teams designed and built AI features into the compliance platform that neither could have delivered as effectively alone.

## Technical Context

- AI feature development within an existing B2B SaaS platform
- Tight integration with regulatory content and domain-specific workflows
- Production-grade implementation, not proof of concept

## Engagement Model

Embedded AI expertise. Zartis engineers worked alongside the client's existing team, collaborating with in-house SMEs to translate regulatory domain knowledge into AI-powered product features.

## Outcome

Accelerated AI adoption within the product. The complementary model — domain expertise inside, AI engineering from Zartis — proved effective for a company with rich content assets but limited AI engineering capacity.
