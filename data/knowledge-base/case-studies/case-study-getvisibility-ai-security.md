# Scaling an AI Data Security Platform from Startup to Acquisition

## The Client

DataShield is an advanced AI-based data security platform operating in one of the most technically challenging areas of enterprise security. The company was later acquired by ClearGuard, a global cybersecurity leader.

## The Problem

Building a team capable of addressing the profound technical and security challenges of enterprise data classification required specialists who could slot in and start delivering quickly. As the platform grew, the internal team needed to scale fast without compromising depth or quality.

In the words of Mark Brennan, Co-Founder and CEO:

> "Scaling a team is difficult, and building a team to meet those challenges needs experts in their field who can slot into and augment the team very, very quickly."

## What Zartis Delivered

Zartis provided senior AI and software engineers who integrated directly into DataShield's team. The focus was on augmenting the core engineering organisation with people who could contribute at a high technical level from day one — not generalists who needed ramping, but specialists who understood the domain.

## Technical Context

- AI-based data classification and security
- Enterprise-grade platform with complex data security requirements
- Embedded team model: Zartis engineers became part of DataShield's own team structure

## Engagement Model

Team augmentation. Zartis engineers embedded directly into the client's organisation, working under the client's processes and alongside their existing team. Engagement scaled with the company's growth trajectory.

## Outcome

The platform achieved the technical depth and team scale required for enterprise credibility and growth. DataShield was subsequently acquired by ClearGuard — a validation of the platform's quality and market position.
