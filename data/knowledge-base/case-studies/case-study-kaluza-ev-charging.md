# Smart EV Charging Platform Cuts Customer Costs 70% and Reduces Grid Peak Demand 55%

## The Client

Voltara is a Meridian Energy Group company — an intelligent platform ecosystem powering the future of energy. Their technology empowers some of the largest energy suppliers to better serve millions of customers, from billing to smart EV charging.

## The Problem

As EV adoption accelerates toward 245 million vehicles by 2030, Voltara needed to build a smart charging platform that would optimise when and how EVs charge. The core challenges were:

- **Grid overload:** Most EV drivers charge overnight when they arrive home, coinciding with peak demand periods. Without intelligent scheduling, this would cause serious grid capacity problems.
- **Cost:** Charging during peak periods means higher electricity costs for EV owners, slowing adoption.
- **Sustainability:** Peak-period charging relies on fossil fuel peaker plants rather than renewables, undermining the environmental case for EVs.

Voltara needed rapid team expansion across multiple product squads to deliver their ambitious vision at speed.

## What Zartis Built

Zartis scaled Voltara's engineering capacity from 4 to nearly 30 engineers embedded across different product squads, bringing expertise in Go, React, Node.js, TypeScript, microservices, and cloud infrastructure. Our engineers played a central role in designing and implementing the real-time power management system at the core of the smart charging platform.

## Technical Approach

- Real-time power management system with sub-second scheduling decisions
- Microservices architecture across multiple product squads
- Cloud-native infrastructure for global scale
- Tech stack: Go, React, Node.js, TypeScript, microservices, cloud platforms

## Engagement Model

4+ year ongoing partnership. Zartis engineers embedded directly into Voltara's product squads — not a separate outsourced team, but part of the core delivery organisation. Team grew from 4 to nearly 30 engineers as the platform expanded.

## Outcomes

- 70% reduction in EV charging costs for customers (up to £500 annually)
- 55% reduction in peak demand through intelligent charging optimisation
- 384 MWh delivered with 215 MWh (56%) shifted away from peak times
- 6.2 tonnes CO2 savings in an 11-month pilot with 300 EV users
- Platform scaled globally across multiple energy providers
